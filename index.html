Questions

Answers, briefly.

Browser autofill fills simple text inputs based on saved form history — it breaks on React-controlled inputs, custom dropdowns, location comboboxes, and file uploads. Rezme uses a purpose-built engine that understands how ATS forms are built: it handles shadow DOM, iframes, native-setter tricks for React, spatial heuristics for option menus, and DataTransfer-based resume attachment. It also writes AI cover letters and scores your resume — browser autofill can't do any of that.
Yes. Workday is notoriously hard because it renders forms inside iframes with custom web components and shadow DOM. Rezme's deepQuery engine traverses those boundaries, and the custom-dropdown handler opens Workday's ARIA comboboxes and clicks the right option by proximity — not just any matching option anywhere on the page.
The AI pulls specific signals from the job description — team name, stated challenges, tech stack, mission language — and matches them against specific details from your profile. The result is specific to the company and sounds like you wrote it, not a chatbot. That said, always read it before inserting — the AI occasionally invents details, and you know your story better than it does.
Your profile data is stored in Chrome's local extension storage — it stays on your device. No profile data is ever sent to our servers. If you use AI features, the relevant job description and profile snippets are sent to Anthropic's API for completion only, and are not retained. You can bring your own Anthropic API key if you want full control.
Rezme uses a universal heuristic engine — not per-ATS modules — so it works on any platform that renders standard HTML form elements, including Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Ashby, SmartRecruiters, iCIMS, Workable, Taleo, BambooHR, JazzHR, Jobvite, Pinpoint, Breezy HR, Bullhorn, and many more. If a new ATS uses standard form patterns, Rezme fills it.
